A disc jockey (DJ) is a person who curates and mixes recorded music for an audience, using equipment to blend songs seamlessly and create a specific atmosphere at clubs, festivals, or private events.

They are considered performers who control the event's vibe, often using turntables, laptops, or media players to create unique sonic experiences.This video explains the basics of DJing, including the equipment use.

The term "disc jockey" was ostensibly coined by radio gossip commentator Walter Winchell in 1935 to describe the radio work of Martin Block. The phrase first appeared in print in a 1941 Variety magazine. Originally, the word "disc" in "disc jockey" referred to phonograph or gramophone records
